The Curious Incident of the Dog in the Night-time

Mark Haddon

When I was first given this book, I did not want to read it. Not because of the book itself or the writing but because it was my novel, I had to write an essay for my exams. However, after three years of it sitting on my bookcase, I decided to read it again. I realised it was a remarkable book, the way Haddon explores the theme of a young boy who has Asperger’s Syndrome. Throughout the book you are not told this, but you realise it when the book chapters are only the prime numbers and the writing itself is simple, but this does not impact the book negatively, it improves it. Each chapter alteration between what is happening in the book and what Christopher is thinking. The book is written in the point of view of Christopher, so it feels like you are experiencing the event within Christopher’s mind.
After my initial uncertainty of this book because it brings back memory of exams, it was a shame I did not read it early. Would highly recommend.
