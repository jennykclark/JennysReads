Girl, Woman, Other

Bernardine Evaristo

Throughout the novel it deals with race, class, privilege, sexuality and gender with the help of 12 characters, who are all interlinked. However, Evaristo does not make the characters feel like vessels for her to delivery her own personal views. With the generational gaps between each character, it makes you want to think about the ideologies change, that each character must experience in their way. It is a must read.
