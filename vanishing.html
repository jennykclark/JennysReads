The Vanishing Half

Brit Bennett

Even though the novel was set decades ago and covers a 30–40-year period. The concept is still relevant for today’s society. The novel itself is a slow burner, but the plot twists keep you reading. It is a character-driven book, two sisters who went their own ways, and their daughters. The way Bennett approaches the heavy themes of social identity, race, class and sexuality makes you start thinking about your own decisions in your life and society. You are reading a book about how impactful decisions can be on ones’ family.
